Why ‘House Hunting’ ?

It's difficult to start a life in the United States as an international student living abroad for the first time, especially if you don't know anyone here. There are numerous difficult challenges. One of them is finding a place to live, which is frequently challenging. There are many things about which we are concerned and skeptical, such as: how about the neighborhood? Is it satisfactory? Is it secure? Can we trust the landlord? We can't even be sure it's true based on what we've read on the internet. Furthermore, we must make decisions outside of the United States, which will have long-term consequences for our lives, or at least one year.

The goal of this study is to enhance the process of settling in for an international student who arrives in a new country without any friends or family.

Lesson Learn

1. Teamwork and communication are important :

Real-world tasks are always more challenging than expected, therefore it's crucial for teams to keep open lines of communication. Work is more efficient when tasks are clearly delegated; for example, if one person conducts interviews while another records them. It's more efficient than trying to do everything by yourself. Without communication and teamwork, we may not do effective interviews and testing.

2. How to test the ideas :

From my previous experience with UX/UI, when it comes to prototyping, it's often remembered to make a digital prototype automatically, and being a digital prototype makes users less involved in testing because there's just an action with clicks and clicks. Personally, it seems like asking a yes-or-no question, which is a type of question that should be avoided for testing in the early stages, a period where we still need more feedback. If time goes back, we'll design a new test, allowing users to be more involved with the test idea. Starting with a whole user question, let the user tell us what they've had to experience when starting house hunting, and then just ask what kind of help they need. Maybe put the user down on paper. Assuming that there is a wonderful app that helps them find a home easier, how would it be more eye-catching than the prototypes that we've built on a minimal basis and experience?

3. Be a better listener :

The interview made me feel like giving a presentation. Of course, we were really focused on explaining too many things, making the user start losing focus and not knowing how to answer questions or talk. It is not a dialogue between two people anymore. In conclusion, unnaturality and unrest may lead to interruptions in the conversation and interview.

4. The quality of the conversation is more valuable than the number of people we interviewed :

The bigger the number of interviewees, the more information they will have. However, the number of interviewees may not be as important as the quality of the conversation. A deep conversation with a person about house hunting may reveal crucial insights that affect our thoughts about our products.

Future Works

After completing the first round of interviews and tests, we noticed that we may have overstretched some of the processes and that there was much to be improved upon.

Step 1: Rethink the problem that we really need to solve or focus on.

people, it's clear that some features are not useful to the user. Some features already exist, and asking the user to adopt new features may not be the solution. We need to review the solution, the objective of the features, and the problem that we would like to solve again.

Through this process, it is necessary to test ideas and collect more data by co-designing so that users can share their home search experiences more naturally, be more involved in the design, and listen to more users. We might repeat this step one more time to make sure that the information that we have covers all kinds of needs for house hunting.

Step 2: Make the prototype the way it's supposed to be based on the information that we have.

Build a new prototype after deciding which problem we want to solve. The prototype could be a paper prototype, a website, a mobile application, or even a social media group.
