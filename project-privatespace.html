Why 'Private Space' ?

First, I want a quiet space because I live in a studio room with my husband and it is really hard to keep my space quiet.

But do I really want quiet space?

No, it's not.

After I think about it again what I really want. If I just want quiet space, I could use noise canceling headphones.

In actually, I just want a private space.

I start to find a space that could be turned to a private space

1. Under the worktable

Private but weird and uncomfortable. It is very hard for humans to work in that space.

2. Under the dining table

Private but weird and uncomfortable. It is very hard for humans to work in that space.

3. Wardrobe

Private, have some space but uncomfortable and don’t have any windows to see the outside world. Even, I want a private space, I still want to see the landscape through the windows and sunbathe while I am working.

What makes me feel ‘private’?

1. No one can see my screen.
2. Feel comfortable to sit and work.
3. I can focus on my work which means it is difficult to be distracted.
4. No people around me are at least 1.5 meters apart. I observed people in the subway. Most people choose to sit apart from another person around 1 seat width which I call buffer seats and they choose to stand if there are no buffer seats. I think the size of the screen affects the buffer space too. If I work on a 24 inch screen, I would require more than 1 subway seat to make me feel comfortable and private.

Observe how people feel the privacy in the different space

Library

I, myself, like to sit in the library because the desks are designed to separate people and make you feel comfortable and have privacy to read or work in the small space.

Subway

I observed people in the subway for a while. They always avoid sitting next to other people. If there is a vacant seat between people, there are 50-50 possibilities that people choose to stand instead of sitting in that seat.

Research the existing solution

I believe that I’m not the only one who has issues about privacy and it’s true. There are a lot of products that were created to solve this problem. In my situation, I live in a studio room with my partner which doesn't have any partitions to separate us. It’s literally an open-small space. I wanted something to separate with my partner temporarily and I found a lot of products inspired from ‘Origami’. It’s beautiful and easy to store it when I don’t use it.

Prototyping

I started to create a few versions of my prototype to explore the different kinds of privacy spaces.

Tent Design

This is a first iteration for privacy space. It’s very private but it is pretty difficult to store when I don’t use it. Plus, I still want to connect to the outside world. I still need sunlight and good ventilation.

Polygon Partition Design

It’s easy to store it but it’s not a privacy space anymore. Other people still can see my screen.

Cocoon Design

This design is inspired from the baby carriage. It looks very comfortable and makes me feel so private.

Fan Design

My key factor is how easy it is to store in my small studio room, then the ‘Fan Design’ pops up in my mind. You just install it between two desks, and when you need some privacy, you just unfold it to create an instant privacy space.

Curtain Wall Design

This is quite basic to me, but it might work well, so I include the ‘Curtain Wall Design’. It is a simple concept, and it is quite easy to use. First, you install it between two desks. Second, you unfold it when you need some space. It seems good, but it is pretty big for my tiny room.

Make it bigger!

After discussing with my classmate, they suggested that I should dig deep into two designs. First is the cocoon design, and second is the fan design. Both designs have the potential to be a product, and they seem to fit real-life situations in the workplace.

Bigger Cocoon Design

I wanted to get closer to the genuine feel of cocoon design, so I chose to make it bigger. Then I grabbed the largest paper roll from stationery and started making it. The origami is pretty challenging when working with 40 x 120 inches. Anyway, I can manage it, and the outcome isn't what I expected. It is too tiny to cover my workspace, but it is large enough to cover my head and laptop. That should give you an idea of how I feel about the cocoon design.

It all works out. It provides me with a sense of privacy! The cocoon design limited my perspective, which reduced distraction and allowed me to focus on my task for longer periods of time.

Bigger Fan Design

To illustrate how it works on a 1:1 scale, I use the same size paper with the larger cocoon design. It works great for me, although there is a 'strength' issue. When unfolded, one must handle it with caution due to its massive size. When I used it for the first time, I accidentally tore it. In any case, the fan design does not handle privacy as well as the cocoon design. Someone could walk by and see what I'm doing on my laptop.

Future Works

There are two points that I would like to work on. First, I would like to find a proper size. Second, I would like to find the proper material to make them stronger.

The size of an instant privacy space isn't just a matter of inches; it's a key factor influencing the user experience. A cramped space can quickly transform feelings of privacy into uncomfortable claustrophobia. Imagine yourself working on a 24-inch desktop computer inside a cocoon designed too small. Your arms brush against the sides, the screen feels uncomfortably close, and instead of being secluded, you feel trapped. Conversely, a space that is too large might feel empty and impersonal, failing to provide the desired sense of retreat. Finding the sweet spot, a size that offers both physical comfort and a feeling of secure enclosure, is crucial for maximizing the effectiveness and appeal of these personal havens. This might range from compact pods ideal for focused tasks to adaptable structures that expand to accommodate larger screens and collaborative work.

Finding the right material is crucial not only for strength and durability but also for creating a truly private and relaxing experience. Multi-layered fabrics with sound-absorbing properties can transform a small space into a peaceful haven, even for users working on larger screens. Honeycomb structures provide exceptional strength without adding bulk, while active noise cancellation technology promises complete sonic isolation. However, practicality should not be ignored. Opting for wipeable surfaces or removable, washable covers ensures your privacy space stays clean and hygienic, while antimicrobial fabrics offer an extra layer of protection in shared environments. Ultimately, the ideal material will strike a balance between strength, soundproofing, ease of cleaning, and sustainability, ensuring your instant privacy space is both functional and inviting.
