The Problem

Visually impaired people can...

Recognize people by listen to their footstep

Walk around using a walking stick and their memory

Recognize object with distinct shape by touching

However, they cannot know the type, brand, color of objects with similar shape by only touch them.

My Role

In this project, my role is to explore user needs and research other products on the market that seek to solve similar problems. When I received the outcome, I had to design it and discuss it with the software and hardware developers to see the possibility of creating it.

After that, a prototype will be built and tested with a group of users to get feedback.

Challenge

The most difficult aspect of creating eyeglasses to assist the blind is that we are not blind. We can try to close our eyes and live for a day to better understand them. But it's difficult to comprehend because we've never lived like them. We'd never been in the dark for as long as they had, so we had to spend a lot of time talking to them. Instead of treating them as subjects, we may need to befriend them and make them feel comfortable enough to tell about their life.

Solution & Features

We intend to concentrate on increasing self-reliance in everyday life after doing study. However, it does not directly address aspects of life safety, despite being a very helpful feature. It only had four functionalities due to limits in the development and implementation of the glasses.

How does it works?

Lesson Learn

We start by imagining what the user wants; for example, we suppose the user wants facial recognition or something similar. However, after speaking with and getting to know our users. They discovered that they could recognize their acquaintances by their walking footfall. It teaches us that knowing the real users keeps us from doing things that the user does not desire. This also clarifies the product's direction.
